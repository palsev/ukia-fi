What is Ukia Trade?

Ukia Trade is a DEX interface for Uniswap v3 contracts. Users can interact with every Uniswap v3 pool and create new ones directly through the Ukia interface.

What is Ukia Analytics?

Ukia Analytics is a Uniswap v3 insights portal. Researchers can find data on all Uniswap v3 pools, tokens, and positions. We recently launched User Analytics, which can be found by searching any address or ENS and lets users view PNL, total swaps, position value, and more.

How do you use Ukia Trade?

The simplest way to use Ukia Trade is to connect your wallet, click on the Swap Page, and select the assets you want to swap, similar to traditional DEX frontends. More advanced users can navigate to the Trade Page, select their preferred pool, and swap within it using market orders or limit orders.

Is Ukia free to use?

Ukia is free to use. The platform does NOT charge a frontend fee on trades, new positions, or pool fee collections. Users only pay gas fees and fees from the associated pool.

Can I cancel a limit order?

Yes, until the order is filled you may cancel the limit order at any time.

How do you deploy an LP position?

Begin by navigating to the Position Maker page and selecting your preferred pool. Now, you can select your range by clicking the chart, selecting a percentage from the dropdown, or manually entering the numbers. Finally, enter the amount of liquidity you’d like to deploy, then deploy it.

How do you view past orders?

Connect your wallet, then navigate to the Orders page on the top right of the platform to view past orders, open orders, and positions.

How do you create a new pool?

Click the settings button on the top right of the terminal and click “Create Pool”. Enter the token addresses of the two assets in the pool, select your fee tier, and the price on deployment. Once you click next, you’ll be prompted to add initial liquidity. After deploying the liquidity, the pool is live on Uniswap v3!

Which chains is Ukia integrated with?

Ukia is built on the existing Uniswap v3 contracts on Ethereum, Polygon, Arbitrum, Optimism, zkSync Era, Rootstock, Boba Network, and Moonbeam.

Which wallets are supported on Ukia?

Metamask, WalletConnect wallets, and all wallets supported by Wallet Connect’s v3 Modal. If your wallet is not on the list, please contact us on Telegram for support.

How do limit orders work?

Limit orders are one-side, one-tick-wide Uniswap v3 positions. Each tick represents a price. Once the tick is crossed, the order is filled, and Chainlink’s Automation system will withdraw the tokens from the position.

Where does Ukia retrieve its data?

Ukia indexes all Uniswap v3 data directly from the chain and propagates it into our custom API. The API is available for other Uniswap ecosystem members to utilize in their applications at no cost.

How does Ukia make money?

Ukia was initially funded by a $1.6m grant from the Uniswap Foundation. On top of that, GFX Labs has other revenue streams, allowing us to not collect revenue from Ukia.

Does Ukia plan to support other chains?

Yes! We are continuously integrating new chains into the interface. If your chain is not currently supported, feel free to contact us on Telegram to make a request.

Has Ukia been audited?

Ukia does not have contracts. The interface interacts with Uniswap v3 contracts, which have been audited. You can review the audit here.
